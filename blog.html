Mecca Cosmetica

10/3/20

Employed by Artitech as the lead draftsman for the installation of Barrisol stretch-ceiling for Mecca Cosmetica’s Sydney flagship store opening on Market street. My brief for this project was to measure up sixteen ceilings, create models in CAD and Adobe Illustrator and liaise with Barrisol in both Melbourne and France. I also worked on-site to install the ceilings, custom-designed and 3D printed hardware and modified drawings in response to unforeseen site conditions.

Swan

10/3/20

Designed in collaboration with Big Kahuna Imagineering and non-for-profit organisation Western Australia Return Recycle Renew (WARRRL). Made from 10,000 recycled bottles - it is a visual representation of how many drink containers are consumed every four minutes in WA. This commissioned piece was created to shine a light on the waste issue in WA and the launch of the “Containers For Change”. The Swan was installed in Perth’s Yagan Square in October 2020.

ROOK

10/3/20

I recently created a web face design for sporting startup Rook. Rook develops bottles specifically designed to be used while wearing boxing gloves. After establishing an objective statement, strategy statement and conducting competitor and audience analysis, a number of options were developed directed towards their target market. San-serif typeface Roboto was chosen to signal a rigid-rhythm appropriate for a combat sport. The colour of Midnight Express couples well with Gray90 and Dark Tangerine creates strong focal points on the screen. Overall this colour pallet conveys a brand of performance and grit. Wireframes and required affordances were then developed before adding media. Rook will launch in 2021 when group boxercise classes return.

Surf Life Saving Australia

10/3/20

Designed in collaboration with Sphere Drones, these custom drone skins enable Surf Lifesaving Australia to identify their fleet of drones using individual asset numbers. The templates were developed for the Phantom 4, Mavic Pro 2 and the Mavic Mini drones. The skins are constructed from a polymeric cast vinyl as its properties allow for it to be wrapped on organic and non-geometric shapes. The decal skins are printed using eco-solvent inks and are matte-laminated to protect them from UV damage, scuffing and moisture before they are die-cut to shape. The skins have now also been customised for Sydney Trains and the Sutherland Shire.

ZENON

10/3/20

ZENON was designed in collaboration with young designers James Anderson and Sasa Barnes as part of the international Next-Gen Mask Competition.

The avant-garde head-piece, aims to reconceptualise the traditional mask and remove the stigma associated with wearing them. With unique collaborations and social exposure, want it to be considered a collectable, akin to the world of the sneaker trade. Driven by social media campaigns we would create excitement around the biannual release of our distinctive and trend challenging masks. We want our design to be constantly evolving, generating high demand for technical protective gear.

We choose our clothes, we choose our accessories and we should be able to choose our masks. They need to be designed as more than just a medical device if you want to get adoption by the masses. ZENON aims to be the first high fashion medical grade mask.
